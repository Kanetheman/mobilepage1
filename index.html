The Connection for Mental Health, PLC is a confidential counseling service. We provide therapy for individuals, adolescents, children, couples, families and groups. The therapists’ treatment approach is based on the knowledge that thoughts, emotions and behaviors are all connected. Our purpose is to help people make this connection in positive ways, which will allow them to live happier more fulfilled lives.

Savannah Crist, LMSW

Trauma • Grief • Perinatal Mental Health • Anxiety • Depression • ADHD • Life Transitions

Savannah is a Licensed Masters Social Worker. She received her Master of Social Work degree from Michigan State University. She has more than ten years experience helping children, adolescents and adults through trauma and grief in a crisis setting. Savannah has training in perinatal mental health, trauma informed parenting, trauma focused cognitive behavioral therapy, play therapy, and motivational interviewing. She also likes working with individuals experiencing anxiety, depression, struggles due to ADHD or life transitions.

scrist@connectmh.com

Megan VanEver, LMSW

Megan is a Licensed Masters Social Worker. She received her Master of Social Work degree from the University of Michigan. Megan has over 8 years of experience in community mental health working with children, adolescents, and families. Megan has training in perinatal mental health, trauma focused cognitive behavioral therapy, trauma informed parenting, motivational interviewing, and play therapy. She enjoys working with individuals experiencing difficulties due to ADHD, anxiety, life transitions, or depression

mvanever@connectmh.com

Janis Duke, LPC

Janis is a licensed professional counselor who has been practicing in Midland for 35 years and has worked with individuals, families, couples, and adolescents. Janis has worked with survivors of domestic violence for the last eight years. She is trained in dialectic behavioral therapy, motivational interviewing, cognitive behavioral therapy, internal family systems and trauma recovery. She enjoys working with Veterans, PTSD, anxiety, depression, and relationship issues. Janis is joined by her certified therapy dog, Hamish, who helps provide comfort and support.

jduke@connectmh.com

Robin L. Greiner, LMSW

Robin is a Licensed Master Social Worker, having received her master’s degree from Saginaw Valley State University. She has over 25 years of experience working as a social worker, with the first 20 years working with those experiencing crisis. She enjoys working with teens and adults who are seeking treatment related to anxiety, depression, life transitions, relationship issues, and trauma. Robin is dedicated to providing a safe, collaborative, and supportive approach to help people reach their therapeutic goals. She is committed to helping those who would like to integrate their spiritual beliefs into therapy.

Robin has specialized training in Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR), motivational interviewing, grief, pain, sleep, and Cognitive Behavior Therapy (CBT).

rgreiner@connectmh.com

Terri Stevenson, MA, LPC

Terri is a Licensed Professional Counselor with a Master’s in Counseling from Central Michigan University. While enjoying an Emeritus status, Terri can be available for returning clients, or for those wanting brief services. Terri has over 30 years of clinical experience and has been voted, "Best Couple & Family Counselor," on multiple occasions for the Great Lakes Bay Region. Her expertise is in relationships; helping people make decisions & improvements in their important relationships. Terri has prepared over, 150 Couples for Marriage and uses the PREPARE/ENRICH premarital inventory in this 4 session model with couples.

Terri is a trained Spiritual Director and is open to incorporating faith beliefs & spirituality in her sessions if the client wishes. She is trained in Mindfulness Stress Reduction and Meditation which has applications for anxiety, trauma, depression and stress management.

tstevenson@connectmh.com

Gary Graham, ACSW, LMSW

Gary Graham is a Licensed Masters level Social Worker with over 30 years clinical experience in the mental health field. This includes working in private practice the past 16 years with 14 years prior experience working in community mental health. Gary enjoys working with individuals, couples and families on a variety of topics. These include work stress, relationship issues, depression, anxiety, grief and loss. Additionally, Gary has ongoing education and experience using therapy approaches including cognitive behavioral therapy, trauma informed therapy, schema therapy, couples therapy, and stress reduction practices including mindfulness based practices. Gary's approach is to emphasize overall individual and relationship wellbeing that incorporates both mental, emotional, and physical health practices.

ggraham@connectmh.com
